Mission Statement

We aim to ease the discomfort of conversations around female specific health issues in children and adolescents. Our goal is to create an environment which is friendly and knowledgeable in the issues our daughters face regarding their growing and changing reproductive systems.

From birth to adulthood, we address their physical and social needs to allow them to grow, learn and perform at their highest potential.

Why Us?

Pediatric Gynecologists are trained in Obstetrics and Gynecology with a focus in the care of girls and teens.

Little Women Pediatric & Adolescent Gynecology, all we see are girls from birth up through college.

We focus on the whole child and how their health affects their overall success in all their endevors.

What makes Dr. Wright the right doctor for you?

Dr. Wright also had a strong desire to learn more about the care of girls with complex and sometimes rare medical concerns. Therefore, she spent an extra year in training to learn about how different complex medical concerns can impact reproductive development and health.

On the other side, she learned how puberty can make a life with these complex concerns even more challenging. Her number one focus is to help all girls with all their unique circumstances transition to a fulfilling and healthy adult life, reaching their highest potential.

Profile

Dr. Stephanie M. Wright, DO, FACOG

Pediatric & Adolescent Gynecology Specialist

Stephanie M. Wright, DO, FACOG is the physician and owner of Pediatric & Adolescent Gynecology of the Rocky Mountains, PC. She first developed an interest in the health care needs of young women as a college student while volunteering with Rotary International’s Rotary Youth Leadership Award (RYLA) girl’s camp for high school juniors and seniors. During this time, it became evident that many young women lacked essential knowledge about self-care and the unique functions of their bodies.

Education and Military Service

After college, Dr. Wright set her sights on becoming a Pediatric and Adolescent Gynecologist. She attended medical school at the University of Health Sciences in Kansas City, Missouri—now known as the Kansas City University of Medicine and Biomedical Sciences. During medical school, she joined the United States Air Force. She completed her internship at David Grant Medical Center at Travis Air Force Base in Northern California and her residency in Obstetrics and Gynecology at the University of California-Davis Medical Center in Sacramento, California.

Following her training, she served an additional three years of active duty as a staff OB/GYN at Malcolm Grow Medical Center at Andrews Air Force Base in Washington, DC.

Specialized Training

In the summer of 2011, Dr. Wright left the military to pursue further specialization in Pediatric and Adolescent Gynecology at Cincinnati Children’s Hospital, one of the leading children’s hospitals in the United States. During her fellowship, she gained experience in treating complex congenital anomalies through the hospital’s renowned Colorectal Center, which collaborates across Pediatric Surgery, Urology, and Gynecology.

Cincinnati Children’s Hospital is one of only fifteen hospitals in the U.S. offering advanced training in Pediatric and Adolescent Gynecology to physicians trained in Obstetrics and Gynecology.

Board Certification and Community Involvement

Dr. Wright is board certified by the American College of Obstetrics and Gynecology with a subspecialty in Pediatric & Adolescent Gynecology recognized by the American Board of OB/GYN. She strives to be both an educational resource and a compassionate supporter for her patients, fostering health and confidence in young women across Colorado.

"Helping girls achieve their highest potential through health."
